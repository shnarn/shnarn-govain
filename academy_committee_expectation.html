Expectation for Academy Committee Member

1. Introduction

This expectation outlines the terms and conditions under which Name: _________________________ agrees to serve as a member of the Academy Committee of Academy Name: _________________________. As a committee member, you are expected to uphold the highest standards of ethical conduct, as defined by the Seven Principles of Public Life (the Nolan Principles), and to comply with all statutory requirements pertaining to your role. Your appointment reflects your commitment to the governance and success of the academy, its students, and the wider community.

2. Commitment to the Nolan Principles

As a member of the Academy Committee, you agree to adhere to the following Seven Principles of Public Life in all your actions and decisions:

  • Selflessness: You will act solely in the public interest, without seeking personal gain or benefits for yourself, your family, or your friends.
  • Integrity: You will avoid placing yourself under any obligation to individuals or organizations that might attempt to influence you improperly in your work.
  • Objectivity: You will make decisions impartially, fairly, and on merit, using the best evidence and without discrimination or bias.
  • Accountability: You will be accountable to the public for your decisions and actions and submit yourself to scrutiny necessary to ensure this.
  • Openness: You will act and make decisions in an open and transparent manner, providing information unless restricted by law or confidentiality.
  • Honesty: You will be truthful in all communications and dealings.
  • Leadership: You will exhibit these principles in your own behaviour, actively promote them, and challenge any poor behaviour that contravenes these standards.

3. Roles and Responsibilities

As a member of the Academy Committee, your primary responsibilities include:

  • Attending all committee meetings and participating actively in discussions and decision-making.
  • Contributing to the development, implementation, and monitoring of the academy’s strategic plan.
  • Reviewing and approving the academy’s budget and financial statements to ensure value for money.
  • Monitoring the academy’s educational performance and outcomes against agreed targets and standards.
  • Supporting and constructively challenging the headteacher and senior leadership team.
  • Participating in the appointment and performance management of the headteacher.
  • Ensuring the academy complies with its funding agreement with the Department for Education and other legal obligations.
  • Undertaking training and development to enhance your effectiveness as a committee member.

4. Code of Conduct

You agree to abide by the following code of conduct:

  • Uphold the Nolan Principles of selflessness, integrity, objectivity, accountability, openness, honesty, and leadership.
  • Maintain strict confidentiality regarding sensitive information discussed in committee meetings, disclosing such information only when legally required or authorized by the committee.
  • Declare any personal, financial, or professional interests that could conflict with your duties as a committee member, and recuse yourself from related discussions or decisions.
  • Refrain from accepting gifts, hospitality, or benefits that could be perceived as influencing your decisions.
  • Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and academy policies relevant to your role.

5. Statutory Duties

As a committee member, you acknowledge your legal responsibilities as both a trustee of the academy trust and a director of the academy trust company. These duties include, but are not limited to:

  • Acting in accordance with the Charities Act 2022, ensuring the academy trust operates in the best interests of its charitable objectives.
  • Fulfilling obligations under company law as a director of the academy trust, a company limited by guarantee.
  • Ensuring the academy adheres to its funding agreement with the Department for Education.
  • Complying with education legislation, including the Education Act 2011, Education and Inspections Act 2006, and other relevant statutes.
  • Meeting obligations under the Equality Act 2010 to promote equality and eliminate discrimination.
  • Safeguarding and promoting the welfare of children, in line with the Children Act 2004 and related guidance.
  • Ensuring compliance with data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Act 2025.

You are expected to familiarize yourself with these legal requirements and seek advice if uncertain about any aspect of your duties.

6. Eligibility

By signing this expectation, you confirm that you are not disqualified from acting as a trustee of a charity or as a company director, as outlined in the Charities Act 2022 and the Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986. This includes, but is not limited to, having no unspent criminal convictions, not being bankrupt, and not being subject to a disqualification order.

7. Term of Office

Your appointment as a committee member is for a term of years, (e.g, 4 years): _________, commencing on start date: ____________, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this expectation or the academy’s articles of association. Your term may be renewed by mutual agreement, subject to the academy’s governance policies.

8. Resignation and Removal

  • Resignation: You may resign from your position by providing X months, (e.g., 2 months): _________ written notice to the chair of the committee.
  • Removal: The committee may remove you from your position if you fail to attend [X, e.g., 3] consecutive meetings without good reason, breach the terms of this expectation, or for other reasons specified in the academy’s articles of association.

9. Confidentiality

You agree to maintain the confidentiality of all information acquired during your tenure as a committee member, except where disclosure is required by law or explicitly authorized by the committee.

10. Conflicts of Interest

You must promptly declare any personal, financial, or professional interests that could conflict with your duties, including relationships with staff, suppliers, or organizations that may engage with the academy. If a conflict arises, you will withdraw from any related discussions or decisions to ensure impartiality.

11. Training and Development

You agree to participate in appropriate training and development opportunities provided by the academy to enhance your ability to perform your duties effectively.

12. Expenses

You may claim reasonable expenses incurred in the course of your duties, such as travel to meetings, in accordance with the academy’s expenses policy.

13. Volunteer Status

Your role as a committee member is voluntary and does not constitute employment with the academy. You will not receive remuneration for your services, except for the reimbursement of approved expenses.

14. Liability and Indemnity

The academy trust maintains insurance to cover your liabilities as a trustee and director, provided you act honestly and in good faith. Further details are outlined in the academy’s articles of association, which provide for indemnity to the extent permitted by law.

15. Amendments

This expectation may be amended by mutual agreement in writing between you and the Academy Committee.
