Timetable for Secondary Link Committee Member Monitoring SEND

A structured guide for overseeing Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in a secondary school

This timetable is designed for a link committee member responsible for monitoring Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in a secondary school. It ensures effective oversight of pupil needs, compliance with the SEND Code of Practice, and alignment with the academic year (Autumn, Spring, Summer terms).

Autumn Term

Committee Meeting (September/October)
  • Set the foundation for SEND monitoring for the year.
  • Review and contribute to the approval of the school's SEND policy and action plan.
  • Examine SEND student data (e.g., number of students, categories of need, new students transitioning from primary school).
  • Ensure the SEND Coordinator provides an update on the identification and assessment of new SEND students.
Monitoring Activities
  • Meet with the SEND Coordinator to discuss how support plans (e.g., Education, Health and Care Plans - EHCPs) are being implemented.
  • Observe a sample of lessons or conduct a learning walk to see how SEND students are supported in practice.
  • Review a selection of EHCPs or support plans to confirm they are current and being followed.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Receive monthly updates from the SEND Coordinator on student progress, staff support, and any issues.
  • Start collecting feedback from staff about SEND training and resource effectiveness.

Spring Term

Committee Meeting (January/February)
  • Assess SEND provision mid-year.
  • Review SEND student progress using assessment data, comparing it to national benchmarks if available.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of support plans and interventions in place.
  • Discuss any additional staff training needs related to SEND.
Monitoring Activities
  • Conduct a focused learning walk or classroom observation targeting SEND provision.
  • Meet with a group of SEND students to hear their feedback on the support they receive.
  • Check the school's SEND information report to ensure it's accurate, up-to-date, and meets statutory requirements.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Continue receiving monthly updates from the SEND Coordinator.
  • Gather feedback from parents and students to spot any concerns or successes.

Summer Term

Committee Meeting (May/June)
  • Review the year's SEND provision and plan ahead.
  • Assess the overall effectiveness of SEND provision using student outcomes, data trends, and feedback.
  • Review how SEND funding and resources have been allocated and used.
  • Help plan for the next academic year, including transitions for SEND students (e.g., to further education or new year groups).
Monitoring Activities
  • Meet with parents of SEND students to get their perspectives on the school's support.
  • Review the school's preparation for upcoming inspections (e.g., Ofsted) with a focus on SEND.
  • Ensure all SEND documentation (e.g., EHCPs, support plans) is compliant and up-to-date.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Support the completion of the annual SEND report for the full committee or governing body.
  • Confirm that all statutory deadlines (e.g., EHCP reviews) have been met.

Throughout the Year

Termly Visits
  • Make at least one visit per term to observe SEND provision (e.g., in classrooms or intervention sessions).
  • Meet with the SEND Coordinator during each visit to discuss updates, challenges, or successes.
Training and Updates
  • Attend training sessions, webinars, or briefings on SEND legislation, best practices, and policy changes.
  • Keep up-to-date with changes to the SEND Code of Practice or other statutory guidance.
Reporting
  • Submit a termly report to the full committee or governing body summarizing your SEND monitoring activities, findings, and recommendations.
  • Ensure any concerns or suggestions are quickly addressed by school leadership.
Statutory Compliance
  • Regularly check the school's compliance with the SEND Code of Practice by:
    • Ensuring all SEND students have appropriate, current support plans.
    • Confirming effective collaboration with external agencies (e.g., local authorities, health services).
    • Verifying that parents and students are involved in decision-making (e.g., via meetings or surveys).
  • Monitor the school's process for identifying and assessing SEND students to ensure it's thorough and timely.
  • Ensure the SEND information report is published on the school's website and updated as required.
Data and Feedback
  • Review termly data on SEND student progress and attainment to identify trends or areas needing attention.
  • Use feedback from staff, students, and parents to evaluate SEND provision effectiveness.
  • Consider comparing the school's SEND support to national standards or other similar schools.

Key Features

  • Pupil Needs: You'll monitor whether SEND students are identified, supported, and making progress through regular data reviews, classroom observations, and direct feedback.
  • Appraisal: You'll assess the effectiveness of SEND provision via termly evaluations, data analysis, and input from stakeholders.
  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and committee meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.
