Health and Safety Monitoring Timetable

For Academy Committee Link Member

This timetable outlines the responsibilities and schedule for an Academy Committee Link Member to monitor Health and Safety in a UK secondary school, ensuring pupil needs are met and statutory requirements are fulfilled.

Autumn Term

September
  • Meet with the School Business Manager and Health and Safety (H&S) Lead to review the school's H&S policy and risk assessments.
  • Confirm compliance with statutory requirements (e.g., fire safety, first aid, COSHH).
  • Review previous H&S inspection reports and action plans.
  • Schedule termly walk-throughs and committee meetings.
  • Conduct a site inspection with the H&S Lead to identify hazards (e.g., classroom conditions, corridors, science labs, PE facilities).
  • Check pupil-specific needs (e.g., accessibility for SEND students, medical plans for pupils with conditions).
  • Verify fire evacuation routes and signage are clear and accessible.
October
  • Confirm staff H&S training records (e.g., fire safety, manual handling, first aid) are up-to-date.
  • Meet with pastoral team to ensure pupil medical needs (e.g., asthma, allergies) are documented and communicated.
  • Attend a staff briefing to promote H&S awareness and gather feedback.
  • Present findings from site walk-through and training review to the Academy Committee.
  • Discuss any outstanding actions and allocate responsibilities.
  • Ensure safeguarding and H&S policies align for pupil wellbeing.
November
  • Observe a fire drill to assess evacuation efficiency and pupil safety.
  • Review lockdown procedures and ensure staff/pupils are briefed.
  • Check first aid kits and defibrillator availability across the site.
  • Sample departmental risk assessments (e.g., science, DT, PE) to ensure they are current and pupil-focused.
  • Verify off-site trip procedures comply with DfE guidance and include pupil-specific risk assessments.
December
  • Meet with H&S Lead to evaluate termly progress against statutory requirements.
  • Prepare a termly H&S report for the Academy Committee, highlighting pupil safety outcomes.
  • Plan priorities for Spring Term (e.g., addressing identified hazards).

Spring Term

January
  • Review updates to national H&S legislation or DfE guidance and ensure school policies reflect these.
  • Check pupil medical records are updated for new starters or changing needs.
  • Conduct a walk-through focusing on high-risk areas (e.g., kitchens, labs, sports facilities).
  • Ensure winter-specific hazards (e.g., icy pathways) are managed.
February
  • Meet with the School Council to gather pupil feedback on H&S concerns (e.g., bullying in communal areas, safe routes).
  • Review staff incident reports to identify trends affecting pupil safety.
  • Audit staff training completion for new or refreshed H&S requirements.
  • Ensure designated first aiders are available during all school hours.
March
  • Liaise with external H&S auditors (if scheduled) to prepare documentation.
  • Review accident and near-miss logs to ensure proper reporting and follow-up.
  • Report to the Academy Committee on Spring Term progress, including pupil feedback and audit readiness.
  • Discuss budget implications for H&S improvements (e.g., equipment upgrades).
April
  • Evaluate termly H&S performance with the H&S Lead, focusing on pupil outcomes.
  • Prepare a termly report for the Academy Committee.
  • Plan Summer Term priorities (e.g., outdoor safety for warmer weather).

Summer Term

May
  • Conduct a site walk-through focusing on outdoor areas and summer-specific risks.
  • Review accident logs for trends and follow up on outstanding actions.
  • Meet with H&S Lead to discuss preparations for the next academic year.
June/July
  • Prepare an annual H&S report for the Academy Committee, summarizing the year's activities and outcomes.
  • Review and update H&S policies and risk assessments for the next year.
  • Ensure all statutory requirements have been met and plan for any summer works.

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Activities and checks are designed to prioritize pupil safety and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and site visits maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability.
