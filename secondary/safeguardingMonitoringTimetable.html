Safeguarding Monitoring Timetable

A Structured Plan for Secondary School Link Academy Committee Members

This timetable is designed for a secondary school link academy committee member to monitor safeguarding effectively throughout the academic year, ensuring compliance with statutory requirements including Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE), the Education Act 2002, the Children Acts 1989 and 2004, and the Independent School Standards applicable to academies.

Autumn Term

Start of Term (September)
  • Review and Approve Child Protection Policy: Examine and approve the updated child protection policy to ensure it reflects current legislation and guidance, such as KCSIE.
  • Ensure Staff Training Completion: Verify that all staff, including new hires, have completed mandatory safeguarding training during induction.
  • Meet with Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL): Discuss the safeguarding priorities and action plan for the academic year with the DSL.
Mid-Term (October/November)
  • Meet with DSL: Review ongoing safeguarding cases and any emerging concerns with the DSL.
  • Review Safeguarding Records: Check a sample of safeguarding records to confirm they are accurate, up to date, and properly maintained.
  • Check Single Central Record (SCR): Ensure the SCR, which logs staff vetting checks, is fully compliant with safer recruitment requirements.
End of Term (December)
  • Report to Governing Body: Attend the governing body meeting to provide an update on safeguarding activities, highlighting any issues or actions taken.

Spring Term

Start of Term (January)
  • Mid-Year Safeguarding Review: Conduct a mid-year evaluation of safeguarding arrangements to assess their effectiveness and identify areas for improvement.
  • Check Online Safety Measures: Confirm that the school's online safety systems, including filtering and monitoring, are in place and effective as per KCSIE requirements.
Mid-Term (February)
  • Meet with DSL: Discuss current safeguarding matters and any updates since the last meeting.
  • Check Staff Training Records: Review records to ensure all staff have received regular safeguarding training updates as required.
End of Term (March)
  • Report to Governing Body: Present findings from the mid-year review to the governing body, including any recommended actions.

Summer Term

Start of Term (April)
  • Review Curriculum for Safeguarding: Observe or evaluate how safeguarding (e.g., online safety, personal safety) is taught within the curriculum, such as through PSHE lessons.
Mid-Term (May)
  • Meet with DSL: Monitor ongoing safeguarding cases and discuss any concerns with the DSL.
  • Review Safeguarding Records: Conduct another check of safeguarding records to ensure continued compliance and accuracy.
End of Term (June/July)
  • Conduct Annual Safeguarding Audit: Lead a comprehensive audit or self-assessment of safeguarding practices, benchmarking against statutory guidance and Ofsted criteria.
  • Review Policy for Next Year: Update the child protection policy for the upcoming academic year based on audit findings and legislative changes.
  • Report to Governing Body: Deliver a detailed report to the governing body on the annual review, outlining successes and plans for improvement.
  • Ensure Staff Training Completion: Confirm that all staff have completed any required safeguarding training before the year ends.

Ongoing Responsibilities

  • Consultation on Complex Cases: Be available to the DSL for advice and support on serious or complex safeguarding incidents as they arise.
  • Standing Agenda Item: Ensure safeguarding is a fixed item on the agenda at every governing body meeting for regular discussion and oversight.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up to date with changes in safeguarding legislation, guidance (e.g., KCSIE updates), and best practices, adapting school practices as needed.
  • Attend Training: Participate in safeguarding and child protection training at induction and regularly thereafter, as mandated by KCSIE for governors and trustees.
  • Website Compliance: Verify that the school's website publishes required safeguarding information, such as the child protection policy and DSL contact details, in line with statutory expectations.

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Activities and checks are designed to prioritize pupil safety and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.

Notes

  • Flexibility: Exact dates for tasks (e.g., mid-term meetings) should be adjusted based on the school's specific term dates and governing body meeting schedule.
  • Academy-Specific Considerations: As an academy, the school must also comply with its funding agreement, which reinforces safeguarding duties. This timetable aligns with those obligations by embedding comprehensive oversight.
