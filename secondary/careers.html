Link Academy Committee Member (Careers) Timetable

Monitoring Careers Provision with Gatsby Benchmarks and Statutory Requirements

This timetable outlines the responsibilities of a Link Academy Committee Member for Careers in a UK secondary school, ensuring effective monitoring and support for careers provision throughout the academic year.

Autumn Term

Start of Term
  • Review the school's careers programme to ensure it is stable, structured, and supported by senior management (Gatsby Benchmark 1).
  • Verify that the careers programme is published on the school's website and accessible to pupils, parents, teachers, and employers, as required by statutory guidance (Gatsby Benchmark 1).
  • Confirm the appointment of a trained Careers Leader responsible for the programme (Gatsby Benchmark 1).
  • Examine destination data from the previous year's leavers to evaluate the programme's impact and identify trends or areas for improvement.
Mid-Term
  • Monitor how pupils and parents access and use career and labour market information, ensuring it is available and understood (Gatsby Benchmark 2).
  • Ensure the careers programme meets the individual needs of all pupils, including those with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) or from disadvantaged backgrounds (Gatsby Benchmark 3).
  • Check that teachers are beginning to link curriculum subjects to careers (Gatsby Benchmark 4).
End of Term
  • Review encounters with employers and employees to assess their frequency and quality (Gatsby Benchmark 5).
  • Ensure plans are in place for workplace experiences later in the year (Gatsby Benchmark 6).
  • Monitor early encounters with further and higher education providers (Gatsby Benchmark 7).
  • Confirm that personal guidance sessions with a qualified careers adviser are scheduled, especially for pupils in key transition years (Years 8-13), as per statutory requirements (Gatsby Benchmark 8).
  • Collect feedback from pupils, parents, teachers, and employers on careers activities to inform future planning.

Spring Term

Start of Term
  • Evaluate the careers programme's effectiveness using stakeholder feedback and data from Compass/Compass+ (Gatsby Benchmark 1).
  • Ensure career and labour market information remains current, relevant, and accessible (Gatsby Benchmark 2).
  • Assess how the programme is tailored to diverse pupil groups (Gatsby Benchmark 3).
Mid-Term
  • Monitor the integration of careers education into the curriculum across subjects (Gatsby Benchmark 4).
  • Support teacher professional development to enhance their ability to link learning to careers.
  • Track the number and quality of employer encounters (Gatsby Benchmark 5).
  • Ensure workplace experiences are scheduled with appropriate risk assessments (Gatsby Benchmark 6).
End of Term
  • Review encounters with further and higher education providers to ensure pupils explore a range of post-school options (Gatsby Benchmark 7).
  • Verify that personal guidance sessions are occurring and documented, meeting the statutory requirement for independent careers advice (Gatsby Benchmark 8).
  • Check compliance with statutory duties, including the Provider Access Legislation and publication of the careers programme.
  • Gather stakeholder feedback to evaluate progress.

Summer Term

Start of Term
  • Conduct a full review of progress against all eight Gatsby Benchmarks using Compass/Compass+.
  • Ensure all statutory requirements (e.g., independent guidance, provider access, and programme publication) are on track for completion.
Mid-Term
  • Monitor the completion of workplace experiences and collect feedback from pupils and employers (Gatsby Benchmark 6).
  • Ensure all pupils receive personal guidance before key transitions (e.g., post-16 or post-18 choices) (Gatsby Benchmark 8).
End of Term
  • Assess the overall effectiveness of the careers programme using Compass/Compass+ data, feedback, and available destination data.
  • Prepare a report for the academy trust or governing body on performance against the Gatsby Benchmarks and statutory requirements.
  • Plan the next year's careers programme, setting new goals based on the evaluation.
  • Collect final stakeholder feedback.

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Activities and checks are designed to prioritize pupil career development and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.

Notes

  • Gatsby Benchmarks: This timetable aligns with all eight benchmarks and statutory requirements for careers provision in England.
  • Flexibility: Adjust the timetable as needed to fit your school's calendar, events, and priorities.
