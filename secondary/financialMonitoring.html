Role: Oversight of financial management to ensure pupil needs are met, statutory requirements are fulfilled, and robust financial monitoring is in place.
Frequency: Monthly, termly, and annual tasks aligned with the academic year (Septemberâ€“August).

Autumn Term

September
  • Meet with the School Business Manager (SBM) and Headteacher to review the approved budget.
  • Ensure budget aligns with the School Improvement Plan (SIP) and prioritizes pupil outcomes (e.g., SEN provision, pupil premium).
  • Verify compliance with Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) requirements.
  • Review and sign off on updated financial policies (e.g., Finance Policy, Charging and Remissions Policy).
  • Ensure policies meet statutory requirements under the Academy Trust Handbook.
  • Review allocation and planned expenditure for Pupil Premium and SEND funding.
  • Confirm plans are evidence-based and focused on improving pupil outcomes.
October
  • Attend Finance Committee meeting to review monthly management accounts.
  • Check income, expenditure, and variances against budget.
  • Ensure pupil-focused spending (e.g., interventions, resources) is on track.
  • Assess financial risks (e.g., funding changes, staffing costs) in the school's risk register.
  • Ensure mitigation strategies are in place and monitored.
November
  • Meet with SBM to prepare for the external audit.
  • Review financial controls, procurement processes, and compliance with ESFA regulations.
  • Review spending on pupil interventions (e.g., tutoring, mental health support).
  • Request evidence of impact (e.g., progress data, attendance).
December
  • Attend meeting to review the external audit findings.
  • Ensure any recommendations are actioned and statutory obligations are met.
  • Review termly financial report with Finance Committee.
  • Confirm alignment with pupil needs and statutory compliance.

Spring Term

January
  • Meet with SBM and Headteacher to assess budget performance.
  • Identify underspends/overspends and agree reallocation to support pupil needs.
February
  • Review monthly management accounts and check for variances.
  • Monitor spending on interventions and resources for impact.
March
  • Prepare for the end-of-year financial review.
  • Ensure all statutory returns are on track for submission.

Summer Term

April
  • Review financial reports to ensure pupil premium funding is being spent appropriately and in line with the strategy.
  • Verify that records of pupil premium eligibility are accurate and up to date.
May
  • Visit the school to observe interventions, focusing on any changes or adjustments made.
  • Meet with staff to discuss progress, challenges, and any additional support required.
June
  • Review Summer term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Begin drafting the impact evaluation for the year in collaboration with the pupil premium coordinator.
  • Ensure preparations are underway for the next academic year's pupil premium strategy.
July
  • Finalise the impact evaluation report, assessing the overall effectiveness of the pupil premium strategy.
  • Contribute to planning the next year's pupil premium strategy based on the evaluation findings.
  • Prepare a final report for the academy committee, including the impact evaluation and recommendations for the future.

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all financial and reporting requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Budgeting and spending are designed to prioritize pupil outcomes and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and committee meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.
