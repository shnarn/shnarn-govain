This timetable is designed for an academy committee to monitor the progress and standards of a secondary school across all subjects for Key Stage 3 (Years 7-9) and Key Stage 4 (Years 10-11). It incorporates statutory checks, such as curriculum compliance, student assessment, and reporting, ensuring comprehensive oversight throughout the academic year.

Autumn Term

Start of Term (September)

Purpose: Establish a baseline and set expectations for the year.

Activities:

  • Review previous year's outcomes, including GCSE results (Key Stage 4) and internal assessments (Key Stage 3).
  • Set academic and pastoral targets for the current year.
  • Confirm curriculum compliance for Key Stage 3 and Key Stage 4 (broad and balanced, including mandated subjects).

Statutory Checks: Curriculum compliance, review of published exam results, and school performance data on the website.

Mid-Term (October/November)

Purpose: Assess initial progress and teaching quality.

Activities:

  • First progress review: Analyze initial assessment data across all subjects and year groups.
  • Teaching quality review: Examine lesson observation summaries.
  • Parental engagement: Verify parents' evenings and progress reports are scheduled.

Statutory Checks: Ensure student progress is assessed and reported to parents as required.

Spring Term

Early Term (January)

Purpose: Focus on Key Stage 4 preparation and support.

Activities:

  • Review Year 11 mock exam results for Key Stage 4 progress.
  • Evaluate intervention plans for underperforming students.
  • Monitor coursework and controlled assessment progress.

Statutory Checks: Confirm GCSE preparation aligns with national standards and exam board regulations.

Mid-Term (February/March)

Purpose: Evaluate mid-year progress and plan ahead.

Activities:

  • Second progress review: Analyze updated assessment data.
  • Curriculum evaluation: Assess effectiveness and plan for next year.
  • Review SEND provision for adequate support and outcomes.
  • Gather student and staff feedback via surveys or focus groups.

Statutory Checks: SEND support, careers education, and equality/diversity policies.

Summer Term

Early Term (April/May)

Purpose: Ensure readiness for external exams and internal assessments.

Activities:

  • Oversee GCSE exam preparations and regulatory compliance.
  • Plan and prepare for Key Stage 3 end-of-year assessments.

Statutory Checks: Verify exam entry processes and safeguarding during exams.

End of Term (June/July)

Purpose: Review year's outcomes and prepare for the next cycle.

Activities:

  • Final progress review: Analyze end-of-year data for Key Stage 3 and preliminary GCSE results.
  • Summarize teaching standards based on observations and professional development.
  • Conduct statutory compliance check: Reporting, website updates, attendance/behavior data.
  • Plan for the next academic year based on outcomes.

Statutory Checks: Full compliance with curriculum, assessment, reporting, safeguarding, SEND, careers, and website requirements.

Key Features of the Timetable

  • Generality: Focuses on whole-school activities, applicable to all subjects, with subject leaders providing supporting reports.
  • Coverage of Key Stages: Addresses Key Stage 3 (internal assessments) and Key Stage 4 (exam preparation and coursework).
  • Statutory Integration: Includes checks on curriculum, assessment, reporting, safeguarding, SEND, equality, careers, and public reporting.
  • Flexibility: Approximate timings allow adaptation to specific school calendars while maintaining a consistent framework.

This timetable enables systematic monitoring of progress and standards, ensuring educational excellence and compliance with UK statutory requirements.
