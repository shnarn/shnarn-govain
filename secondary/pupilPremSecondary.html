Pupil Premium Link Committee Timetable

Academic Year: September to July

This timetable outlines the responsibilities of a Pupil Premium Link Committee Member in a UK secondary school, ensuring effective monitoring and support for pupil premium students throughout the academic year.

Autumn Term

September
  • Review and finalise the pupil premium strategy for the academic year, ensuring it aligns with the school's overall development plan.
  • Ensure the strategy is published on the school website as per statutory requirements.
  • Meet with the pupil premium coordinator to discuss implementation plans and confirm alignment with school priorities.
October
  • Visit the school to observe pupil premium interventions in action.
  • Meet with staff delivering interventions to understand their roles and identify any support needs.
November
  • Review Autumn term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Compare their progress and attainment with non-pupil premium peers and national benchmarks.
  • Discuss findings with the pupil premium coordinator and identify any necessary adjustments to interventions.
December
  • Prepare a report for the academy committee on the progress of pupil premium interventions, based on observations and data.
  • Attend the academy committee meeting to present the report and address any concerns or questions.

Spring Term

January
  • Ensure any agreed adjustments to interventions from the previous term are being implemented effectively.
  • Observe any new interventions starting in the Spring term.
  • Check that staff delivering interventions have received necessary training; observe a training session if possible.
February
  • Meet with a focus group of pupil premium students to gather feedback on the support they are receiving.
  • Optionally, meet with parents of pupil premium students (e.g., during a parents' evening) to gather their perspectives.
March
  • Review Spring term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Evaluate the impact of interventions and discuss with the pupil premium coordinator any adjustments needed for the Summer term.
  • Prepare a mid-year report for the academy committee summarising progress and impact.

Summer Term

April
  • Review financial reports to ensure pupil premium funding is being spent appropriately and in line with the strategy.
  • Verify that records of pupil premium eligibility are accurate and up to date.
May
  • Visit the school to observe interventions, focusing on any changes or adjustments made.
  • Meet with staff to discuss progress, challenges, and any additional support required.
June
  • Review Summer term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Begin drafting the impact evaluation for the year in collaboration with the pupil premium coordinator.
  • Ensure preparations are underway for the next academic year's pupil premium strategy.
July
  • Finalise the impact evaluation report, assessing the overall effectiveness of the pupil premium strategy.
  • Contribute to planning the next year's pupil premium strategy based on the evaluation findings.
  • Prepare a final report for the academy committee, including the impact evaluation and recommendations for the future.

Ongoing Activities (Throughout the Year)

  • Receive regular updates from the pupil premium coordinator (e.g., monthly or half-termly via email or brief meetings) to stay informed of progress and any emerging issues.
  • Be available to address any urgent matters related to pupil premium funding or interventions.
  • Ensure the pupil premium strategy remains integrated with the school's overall development plan and is prepared for external scrutiny (e.g., Ofsted inspections).

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Activities and checks are designed to prioritize pupil premium student progress and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.

Notes

  • Flexibility: This timetable is a general guide and can be adjusted based on the school's specific assessment cycles, academy committee meeting schedule, or unexpected needs (e.g., additional visits if data highlights concerns).
  • Statutory Compliance: The timetable ensures compliance with requirements such as publishing the pupil premium strategy in September and evaluating its impact annually, which informs the following year's strategy.
