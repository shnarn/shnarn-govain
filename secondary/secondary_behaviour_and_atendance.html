Academy Committee Timetable for Monitoring Behaviour and Attendance

This timetable is designed for the academy committee, with a specific focus on the link governor for behaviour and attendance, to ensure robust oversight of these critical areas in a UK secondary school. It aligns with the academic calendar (Autumn, Spring, Summer terms) and addresses pupil needs, including those of SEND, EAL, and disadvantaged pupils (e.g., Pupil Premium), through targeted monitoring and interventions. The timetable ensures compliance with statutory requirements, including the Education Act 2002 (Section 175 for safeguarding, Section 51A for exclusions), the Education Act 2011 (Sections 2, 5, 38, 56 for discipline and governance), and the Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006 for attendance. It incorporates Ofsted expectations, safeguarding protocols, and age-appropriate strategies for secondary pupils, such as restorative practices and exam-related support, while facilitating staff appraisals and parental engagement to promote a positive school environment.

Autumn Term (September - December)

September

  • Week 1: Committee Kick-Off Meeting
    • Review previous year's behaviour and attendance data (e.g., attendance rates, exclusion statistics, behaviour incident logs).
    • Agree on key performance indicators (KPIs) for the year, e.g., target attendance rate >=96%, reduction in persistent absenteeism to below 10%.
    • Assign link governor to liaise with Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) and Attendance Officer.
    • Ensure compliance with statutory attendance reporting (e.g., daily registers, Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006).
  • Week 3: Policy Review
    • Review and update Behaviour and Attendance Policies to align with DfE guidance, Education Act 2002 (Section 175 for safeguarding), and Education Act 2011 (Sections 2 and 5 for discipline powers, e.g., same-day detentions, pupil searches).
    • Ensure policies address pupil needs, including SEND, EAL, and disadvantaged pupils (Pupil Premium), with a focus on adolescent-specific strategies like restorative justice.

October

  • Week 2: Link Governor School Visit
    • Observe behaviour in classrooms, corridors, and communal areas (e.g., break times, lunch).
    • Meet with pastoral leads to discuss early interventions for at-risk pupils, such as mentoring or behaviour contracts.
    • Check SEND and EAL pupil support plans for tailored behaviour and attendance adjustments.
  • Week 4: Data Analysis Meeting
    • Analyse half-termly attendance data (e.g., unauthorised absences, lateness trends).
    • Review behaviour incident reports, including fixed-term or permanent exclusions, ensuring compliance with Education Act 2002 (Section 51A) and Education Act 2011 (Independent Review Panels).
    • Verify that exclusion processes follow DfE guidance (2012, as revised).

November

  • Week 1: Parental Engagement Review
    • Evaluate effectiveness of communication with parents/carers on attendance and behaviour issues, e.g., parent meetings, letters, or online portals.
    • Ensure home-school agreements are signed and understood, particularly for pupils with persistent issues.
  • Week 3: Staff Appraisal Input
    • Provide feedback to senior leadership on pastoral staff performance regarding behaviour and attendance strategies.
    • Review training needs for staff on behaviour management (e.g., de-escalation techniques) and attendance monitoring.

December

  • Week 2: Termly Committee Meeting
    • Report on Autumn term progress against KPIs.
    • Discuss safeguarding concerns linked to attendance (e.g., Children Missing Education, CME), per Education Act 2002 (Section 175).
    • Prepare for Ofsted inspection readiness, ensuring evidence of robust monitoring (e.g., behaviour logs, attendance records).

Spring Term (January - March)

January

  • Week 1: Link Governor Meeting with Attendance Officer
    • Review post-holiday attendance trends and interventions for persistent absentees, focusing on vulnerable groups (e.g., Year 7 transitions, Year 11 exam preparation).
    • Ensure compliance with local authority reporting for CME and safeguarding, per Education Act 2002 (Section 175).
  • Week 3: Pupil Voice Session
    • Conduct focus group with pupils (e.g., student council) to gather feedback on behaviour policies and school environment.
    • Ensure pupil needs (e.g., mental health, bullying prevention) are addressed in policy implementation, with emphasis on adolescent wellbeing.

February

  • Week 2: Behaviour Strategy Review
    • Evaluate effectiveness of behaviour interventions (e.g., restorative practices, detentions, peer mentoring).
    • Check support for pupils with SEND or EHCPs to meet statutory requirements, ensuring adjustments are appropriate for secondary pupils.
  • Week 4: Link Governor School Visit
    • Observe implementation of behaviour policies during lessons, transitions, and extracurricular activities.
    • Meet with DSL to review safeguarding links to attendance and behaviour, ensuring compliance with Education Act 2002 (Section 175).

March

  • Week 1: Data and Intervention Review
    • Analyse Spring term attendance and behaviour data, focusing on trends in persistent absenteeism or recurring incidents.
    • Discuss escalation processes for pupils with persistent issues (e.g., referrals to Education Welfare Officer or external agencies).
  • Week 3: Termly Committee Meeting
    • Review progress against KPIs and adjust strategies as needed.
    • Ensure statutory reporting (e.g., exclusion data to DfE) is completed, per Education Act 2011.

Summer Term (April - July)

April

  • Week 1: Link Governor Meeting with Pastoral Leads
    • Discuss summer term priorities, including transition planning for Year 11 (post-GCSE) and Year 7 (new starters).
    • Review summer term attendance incentives (e.g., rewards for 100% attendance, recognition assemblies).
  • Week 3: Policy Compliance Check
    • Ensure behaviour and attendance policies align with updated DfE guidance, Education Act 2002 (Section 175), and Education Act 2011 (Sections 2, 5, 38, 56 for discipline and governance).
    • Verify that statutory registers and records are maintained (Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006).

May

  • Week 2: Link Governor School Visit
    • Focus on Year 11 behaviour and attendance during GCSE exam season and Year 7 adjustment to secondary routines.
    • Review interventions for vulnerable pupils (e.g., Looked After Children, Pupil Premium).
  • Week 4: Staff Training Review
    • Evaluate staff training on behaviour management (e.g., managing challenging adolescent behaviour) and attendance monitoring.
    • Recommend additional CPD, such as training on mental health support or conflict resolution.

June

  • Week 1: Data Analysis Meeting
    • Review summer term data, focusing on trends in exclusions and persistent absenteeism.
    • Ensure compliance with safeguarding and attendance reporting requirements, per Education Act 2002 (Section 175).
  • Week 3: Pupil and Parent Feedback
    • Gather feedback via pupil surveys or parent meetings to assess perceptions of behaviour and attendance policies.
    • Address any concerns raised by pupils or parents, particularly for SEND or disadvantaged families.

July

  • Week 1: Annual Review Meeting
    • Present annual report on behaviour and attendance to full governing board, highlighting progress against KPIs.
    • Evaluate overall progress and set targets for next academic year.
    • Ensure all statutory requirements (e.g., safeguarding, exclusions, attendance) are met, per Education Acts 2002 and 2011.
  • Week 2: Transition Planning
    • Review support plans for incoming Year 7 pupils with behaviour or attendance concerns.
    • Ensure handover of records for Year 11 leavers complies with data protection laws (e.g., GDPR).

Ongoing Responsibilities

Link Governor Role

    • Monthly check-ins with Attendance Officer and DSL to monitor trends and interventions.
    • Termly school visits to observe policy implementation and pupil wellbeing.
    • Act as point of contact for parental concerns escalated to governors.

Committee Responsibilities

    • Ensure all policies comply with DfE and Ofsted requirements, with a focus on secondary-specific approaches (e.g., restorative practices, exam stress support).
    • Monitor impact of interventions on pupil outcomes, particularly for SEND, EAL, and disadvantaged pupils.
    • Maintain records for Ofsted inspections, demonstrating robust oversight of behaviour and attendance.

Statutory Compliance Checklist

    • Daily attendance registers maintained (Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006).
    • Exclusion processes follow DfE guidance, Education Act 2002 (Section 51A), and Education Act 2011 (Independent Review Panels).
    • Safeguarding concerns linked to attendance reported to local authority (Education Act 2002, Section 175).
    • Annual report to governors includes behaviour and attendance data, reflecting Education Act 2011 governance requirements (Sections 38, 56).
