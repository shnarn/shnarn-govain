SEND Link Committee Member Timetable

A structured guide for overseeing Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in a primary school

This timetable is designed for a SEND link committee member (often referred to as a link governor) responsible for monitoring Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in a primary school. It ensures that pupil needs are identified, met, and appraised, while fulfilling statutory requirements outlined in the SEND Code of Practice. The academic year is divided into three terms: Autumn (September–December), Spring (January–March/April), and Summer (April/May–July).

Autumn Term

Committee Meeting (September/October)
  • Establish the SEND monitoring framework for the year.
  • Review and contribute to the approval of the school's SEND policy and action plan, ensuring it addresses the needs of younger children.
  • Examine SEND student data, focusing on early identification of needs, especially for new Reception students and those transitioning from nursery settings.
  • Ensure the SEND Coordinator provides an update on the assessment process for new students, including those with emerging needs.
Monitoring Activities
  • Meet with the SEND Coordinator to discuss how support plans (e.g., Education, Health and Care Plans - EHCPs, or individual support plans) are being developed for identified students.
  • Observe a sample of lessons or conduct a learning walk to see how SEND students are supported, particularly in early years and Key Stage 1.
  • Review a selection of support plans to ensure they are age-appropriate and tailored to each child's developmental stage.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Receive monthly updates from the SEND Coordinator on student progress, early interventions, and any concerns.
  • Begin gathering feedback from teachers and teaching assistants about the effectiveness of SEND training and resources, especially for early years staff.

Spring Term

Committee Meeting (January/February)
  • Conduct a mid-year evaluation of SEND provision.
  • Review SEND student progress using age-appropriate assessment data (e.g., phonics screening, early literacy and numeracy skills).
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of early interventions and support plans, ensuring they are helping students meet developmental milestones.
  • Discuss any additional training needs for staff, particularly in identifying and supporting emerging needs in younger children.
Monitoring Activities
  • Conduct a focused learning walk or classroom observation, paying special attention to how SEND students are supported in play-based learning or small group activities.
  • Meet with a group of SEND students (where appropriate) or observe their engagement in lessons to assess their comfort and progress.
  • Check the school's SEND information report to ensure it is parent-friendly, up-to-date, and meets statutory requirements.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Continue receiving monthly updates from the SEND Coordinator.
  • Collect feedback from parents, particularly those of younger children, to ensure they feel supported and involved in their child's education.

Summer Term

Committee Meeting (May/June)
  • Evaluate the year's SEND provision and prepare for the next academic year.
  • Assess the overall effectiveness of SEND provision using student outcomes, developmental progress, and feedback from staff and parents.
  • Review the allocation and use of SEND funding and resources, ensuring they are being used efficiently to support early intervention.
  • Help plan for transitions, such as students moving from Reception to Key Stage 1 or from Key Stage 2 to secondary school, ensuring SEND students are well-prepared.
Monitoring Activities
  • Meet with parents of SEND students to gather their perspectives on the school's support and communication.
  • Review the school's preparation for any upcoming inspections (e.g., Ofsted), with a focus on how SEND is addressed in early years and across the school.
  • Ensure all SEND documentation (e.g., EHCPs, support plans) is up-to-date and compliant.
Ongoing Responsibilities
  • Support the completion of the annual SEND report for the full committee or governing body.
  • Confirm that all statutory deadlines (e.g., EHCP reviews) have been met and that early identification processes are in place for the next academic year.

Throughout the Year

Termly Visits
  • Make at least one visit per term to observe SEND provision in action, such as in early years classrooms, intervention groups, or during playtime for social development.
  • Meet with the SEND Coordinator during each visit to discuss updates, challenges, or successes, with a focus on early intervention and parental involvement.
Training and Updates
  • Attend training sessions, webinars, or briefings on SEND legislation, best practices for primary schools, and early identification strategies.
  • Stay informed about changes to the SEND Code of Practice or other statutory guidance, particularly as they relate to younger children.
Reporting
  • Submit a termly report to the full committee or governing body summarizing your SEND monitoring activities, findings, and recommendations.
  • Ensure any concerns, especially those related to early identification or parental engagement, are promptly addressed by school leadership.
Statutory Compliance
  • Regularly check the school's compliance with the SEND Code of Practice by:
    • Ensuring all SEND students have appropriate, current support plans that reflect their developmental needs.
    • Confirming effective collaboration with external agencies (e.g., speech and language therapists, educational psychologists) for early intervention.
    • Verifying that parents are actively involved in decision-making, with regular meetings and clear communication channels.
  • Monitor the school's process for identifying and assessing SEND students, ensuring it is thorough, timely, and sensitive to the needs of younger children.
  • Ensure the SEND information report is published on the school's website and updated as required, with language that is accessible to parents.
Data and Feedback
  • Review termly data on SEND student progress, focusing on developmental milestones, social skills, and early academic achievements.
  • Use feedback from staff, students (where appropriate), and parents to evaluate the effectiveness of SEND provision.
  • Consider comparing the school's SEND support to national standards or other similar primary schools to identify areas for improvement.

Key Features

  • Pupil Needs: You'll monitor whether SEND students, particularly younger children, are identified early, supported appropriately, and making progress through regular data reviews, classroom observations, and direct feedback.
  • Appraisal: You'll assess the effectiveness of SEND provision via termly evaluations, developmental data analysis, and input from stakeholders, with a focus on early intervention.
  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and committee meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.

Notes

  • Flexibility: This timetable is a flexible guide—feel free to adjust it based on your school's specific needs or unexpected events (e.g., an Ofsted inspection).
  • Statutory Requirements: Compliance checks are built into your activities, ensuring EHCPs, funding, documentation, and parental involvement meet legal standards.
