Academy Committee Timetable for Monitoring Behaviour and Attendance

This timetable outlines the responsibilities and schedule for the academy committee, with a focus on the link governor for behaviour and attendance, to ensure pupil needs are met, statutory requirements are fulfilled, and effective monitoring and appraisal processes are in place. It aligns with UK primary school academic terms and incorporates Ofsted expectations, safeguarding requirements, Education Act 2002 (Section 175 for safeguarding), and Education Act 2011 (Sections 2, 5, 38, 56 for discipline and governance).

Autumn Term (September - December)

September

Week 1: Committee Kick-Off Meeting
  • Review previous year's behaviour and attendance data (e.g., attendance rates, behaviour incident logs, exclusion statistics).
  • Agree on key performance indicators (KPIs) for the year, e.g., target attendance rate >=96%, reduction in persistent absenteeism to below 8%.
  • Assign link governor to liaise with Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) and Attendance Officer.
  • Ensure compliance with statutory attendance reporting (e.g., daily registers, Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006).
Week 3: Policy Review
  • Review and update Behaviour and Attendance Policies to align with DfE guidance, Education Act 2002 (Section 175 for safeguarding), and Education Act 2011 (Sections 2 and 5 for discipline powers).
  • Ensure policies address pupil needs, including SEND, EAL, and disadvantaged pupils (Pupil Premium), with a focus on positive reinforcement suitable for primary-aged children.

October

Week 2: Link Governor School Visit
  • Observe behaviour in classrooms, playgrounds, and during transitions (e.g., assembly, lunch).
  • Meet with pastoral leads to discuss early interventions, such as nurture groups or social skills sessions.
  • Check SEND and EAL pupil support plans for tailored behaviour and attendance strategies.
Week 4: Data Analysis Meeting
  • Analyse half-termly attendance data (e.g., unauthorised absences, lateness trends, particularly for Reception and KS1 pupils).
  • Review behaviour incident reports, focusing on low-level disruptions and rare exclusion cases, ensuring compliance with Education Act 2002 (Section 51A) and Education Act 2011.
  • Verify that exclusion processes, if used, follow DfE guidance (2012, as revised).

November

Week 1: Parental Engagement Review
  • Evaluate effectiveness of communication with parents/carers, e.g., attendance letters, parent workshops, or home-school agreements.
  • Ensure parents of SEND or vulnerable pupils are supported with clear attendance and behaviour expectations.
Week 3: Staff Appraisal Input
  • Provide feedback to senior leadership on pastoral staff performance in implementing behaviour and attendance strategies.
  • Review training needs for staff on age-appropriate behaviour management and attendance monitoring.

December

Week 2: Termly Committee Meeting
  • Report on Autumn term progress against KPIs.
  • Discuss safeguarding concerns linked to attendance (e.g., Children Missing Education, CME), per Education Act 2002 (Section 175).
  • Prepare for Ofsted inspection readiness, ensuring evidence of robust monitoring.

Spring Term (January - March)

January

Week 1: Link Governor Meeting with Attendance Officer
  • Review post-holiday attendance trends, focusing on vulnerable groups.
  • Ensure compliance with local authority reporting for CME and safeguarding, per Education Act 2002 (Section 175).
Week 3: Pupil Voice Session
  • Conduct age-appropriate pupil feedback sessions (e.g., circle time with KS2 pupils) to gather views on school environment and behaviour policies.
  • Ensure pupil needs (e.g., emotional wellbeing, bullying prevention) are addressed.

February

Week 2: Behaviour Strategy Review
  • Evaluate effectiveness of behaviour interventions (e.g., reward systems, time-out procedures, nurture groups).
  • Check support for pupils with SEND or EHCPs to meet statutory requirements.
Week 4: Link Governor School Visit
  • Observe implementation of behaviour policies during lessons, playtimes, and assemblies.
  • Meet with DSL to review safeguarding links to attendance and behaviour.

March

Week 1: Data and Intervention Review
  • Analyse Spring term attendance and behaviour data, focusing on trends in persistent absenteeism or recurring low-level disruptions.
  • Discuss escalation processes for pupils with persistent issues.
Week 3: Termly Committee Meeting
  • Review progress against KPIs and adjust strategies as needed.
  • Ensure statutory reporting (e.g., exclusion data to DfE, if applicable) is completed.

Summer Term (April - July)

April

Week 1: Link Governor Meeting with Pastoral Leads
  • Discuss summer term priorities, including transition planning for Year 6 and Reception.
  • Review summer term attendance incentives (e.g., certificates, class rewards).
Week 3: Policy Compliance Check
  • Ensure behaviour and attendance policies align with updated DfE guidance and statutory requirements.
  • Verify that statutory registers and records are maintained.

May

Week 2: Link Governor School Visit
  • Focus on Year 6 behaviour and attendance during transition preparations and KS1 pupils during SATs.
  • Review interventions for vulnerable pupils.
Week 4: Staff Training Review
  • Evaluate staff training on behaviour management and attendance monitoring.
  • Recommend additional CPD, such as training on trauma-informed approaches.

June

Week 1: Data Analysis Meeting
  • Review summer term data, focusing on trends in exclusions and persistent absenteeism.
  • Ensure compliance with safeguarding and attendance reporting requirements.
Week 3: Pupil and Parent Feedback
  • Gather feedback via age-appropriate pupil surveys or parent meetings.
  • Address any concerns raised by pupils or parents, particularly for SEND or disadvantaged families.

July

Week 1: Annual Review Meeting
  • Present annual report on behaviour and attendance to full governing board, highlighting progress against KPIs.
  • Evaluate overall progress and set targets for next academic year.
  • Ensure all statutory requirements are met.
Week 2: Transition Planning
  • Review support plans for incoming Reception pupils with behaviour or attendance concerns.
  • Ensure handover of records for Year 6 leavers complies with data protection laws (e.g., GDPR).

Ongoing Responsibilities

Link Governor Role
  • Monthly check-ins with Attendance Officer and DSL to monitor trends and interventions.
  • Termly school visits to observe policy implementation and pupil wellbeing.
  • Act as point of contact for parental concerns escalated to governors.
Committee Responsibilities
  • Ensure all policies comply with DfE and Ofsted requirements, with a focus on primary-specific approaches.
  • Monitor impact of interventions on pupil outcomes, particularly for SEND, EAL, and disadvantaged pupils.
  • Maintain records for Ofsted inspections, demonstrating robust oversight of behaviour and attendance.

Statutory Compliance Checklist

  • Daily attendance registers maintained (Education (Pupil Registration) Regulations 2006).
  • Exclusion processes, if used, follow DfE guidance, Education Act 2002 (Section 51A), and Education Act 2011.
  • Safeguarding concerns linked to attendance reported to local authority (Education Act 2002, Section 175).
  • Annual report to governors includes behaviour and attendance data, reflecting Education Act 2011 governance requirements.
