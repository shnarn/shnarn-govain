Safeguarding Monitoring Timetable

A structured plan for monitoring safeguarding in a primary school

This timetable is designed for a Primary School Link Academy Committee Member to monitor safeguarding effectively throughout the academic year. It ensures compliance with statutory requirements, including Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE), the Education Act 2002, the Children Acts 1989 and 2004, and the Independent School Standards applicable to academies. The timetable is structured around a three-term academic year (Autumn, Spring, and Summer) and includes scheduled tasks and ongoing responsibilities to maintain a robust safeguarding framework tailored to primary school children.

Autumn Term

Start of Term (September)
  • Review and Approve Child Protection Policy: Examine and approve the updated child protection policy to ensure it reflects current legislation and guidance, such as KCSIE, and is appropriate for primary-aged children.
  • Ensure Staff Training Completion: Verify that all staff, including new hires, have completed mandatory safeguarding training during induction, with a focus on recognizing signs of abuse in younger children.
  • Meet with Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL): Discuss the safeguarding priorities for the academic year, including any specific concerns related to primary school students (e.g., early intervention for emotional well-being).
Mid-Term (October/November)
  • Meet with DSL: Review ongoing safeguarding cases and any emerging concerns, particularly around early signs of neglect or emotional abuse, which may be more subtle in younger children.
  • Review Safeguarding Records: Check a sample of safeguarding records to ensure they are accurate, up to date, and properly maintained, with attention to records of low-level concerns that may indicate emerging issues.
  • Check Single Central Record (SCR): Ensure the SCR, which logs staff vetting checks, is fully compliant with safer recruitment requirements, including checks for volunteers and regular visitors.
End of Term (December)
  • Report to Governing Body: Attend the governing body meeting to provide an update on safeguarding activities, highlighting any issues or actions taken, with a focus on the well-being of primary-aged children.

Spring Term

Start of Term (January)
  • Mid-Year Safeguarding Review: Conduct a mid-year evaluation of safeguarding arrangements to assess their effectiveness, with particular attention to how well the school supports the emotional and social development of young children.
  • Check Online Safety Measures: Confirm that the school's online safety systems, including filtering and monitoring, are in place and effective, ensuring they are age-appropriate for primary students as per KCSIE requirements.
Mid-Term (February)
  • Meet with DSL: Discuss current safeguarding matters, including any updates on early help interventions or family support initiatives.
  • Check Staff Training Records: Review records to ensure all staff have received regular safeguarding training updates, including training on age-specific risks such as child-on-child abuse in younger children.
End of Term (March)
  • Report to Governing Body: Present findings from the mid-year review to the governing body, including any recommended actions to enhance safeguarding for primary students.

Summer Term

Start of Term (April)
  • Review Curriculum for Safeguarding: Observe or evaluate how safeguarding is taught within the curriculum, ensuring that topics like personal safety, online safety, and emotional well-being are delivered in an age-appropriate manner (e.g., through PSHE lessons).
Mid-Term (May)
  • Meet with DSL: Monitor ongoing safeguarding cases and discuss any concerns, with a focus on preparing for transitions (e.g., Year 6 students moving to secondary school).
  • Review Safeguarding Records: Conduct another check of safeguarding records to ensure continued compliance and accuracy, paying attention to records of early help and multi-agency working.
End of Term (June/July)
  • Conduct Annual Safeguarding Audit: Lead a comprehensive audit or self-assessment of safeguarding practices, benchmarking against statutory guidance and Ofsted criteria, with specific consideration of the needs of primary-aged children.
  • Review Policy for Next Year: Update the child protection policy for the upcoming academic year based on audit findings and any changes in legislation or guidance.
  • Report to Governing Body: Deliver a detailed report to the governing body on the annual review, outlining successes and plans for improvement, particularly in areas like parental engagement and community partnerships.
  • Ensure Staff Training Completion: Confirm that all staff have completed any required safeguarding training before the year ends, including refreshers on recognizing signs of abuse in young children.

Ongoing Responsibilities

  • Consultation on Complex Cases: Be available to the DSL for advice and support on serious or complex safeguarding incidents, such as those involving very young children or allegations against staff.
  • Standing Agenda Item: Ensure safeguarding is a fixed item on the agenda at every governing body meeting for regular discussion and oversight, with updates tailored to the primary school context.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up to date with changes in safeguarding legislation, guidance (e.g., KCSIE updates), and best practices, particularly those relevant to primary education, such as early years safeguarding.
  • Attend Training: Participate in safeguarding and child protection training at induction and regularly thereafter, as mandated by KCSIE for governors and trustees, with a focus on the unique aspects of safeguarding in primary schools.
  • Website Compliance: Verify that the school's website publishes required safeguarding information, such as the child protection policy and DSL contact details, in line with statutory expectations and in a way that is accessible to parents of young children.

Notes

  • Statutory Compliance: This timetable ensures adherence to key statutory requirements, including maintaining an effective child protection policy, staff training, safer recruitment (via SCR checks), record-keeping, curriculum inclusion of safeguarding, and regular monitoring and review, as outlined in KCSIE and related legislation. It is tailored to the primary school context, with an emphasis on early intervention and age-appropriate education.
  • Flexibility: Exact dates for tasks (e.g., mid-term meetings) should be adjusted based on the school's specific term dates and governing body meeting schedule.
  • Academy-Specific Considerations: As an academy, the school must also comply with its funding agreement, which reinforces safeguarding duties. This timetable aligns with those obligations by embedding comprehensive oversight tailored to primary education.
