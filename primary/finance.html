Finance Monitoring Timetable

For Academy Committee Link Member (Primary School)

Role: Oversight of financial management to ensure pupil needs are met, statutory requirements are fulfilled, and robust financial monitoring is in place.
Frequency: Monthly, termly, and annual tasks aligned with the academic year (Septemberâ€“August).

Autumn Term

September
  • Meet with the School Business Manager (SBM) and Headteacher to review the approved budget.
  • Ensure budget aligns with the School Improvement Plan (SIP) and prioritizes pupil outcomes (e.g., Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) provision, pupil premium).
  • Verify compliance with Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) requirements.
  • Review and sign off on updated financial policies (e.g., Finance Policy, Charging and Remissions Policy).
  • Ensure policies meet statutory requirements under the Academy Trust Handbook.
  • Review allocation and planned expenditure for Pupil Premium and SEND funding.
  • Confirm plans are evidence-based and focus on improving pupil outcomes (e.g., phonics support, early intervention).
October
  • Attend Finance Committee meeting to review monthly management accounts.
  • Check income, expenditure, and variances against budget.
  • Ensure pupil-focused spending (e.g., EYFS resources, reading interventions) is on track.
  • Assess financial risks (e.g., funding changes, staffing costs) in the school's risk register.
  • Ensure mitigation strategies are in place and monitored.
November
  • Meet with SBM to prepare for the external audit.
  • Review financial controls, procurement processes, and compliance with ESFA regulations.
  • Review spending on pupil interventions (e.g., speech and language support, emotional wellbeing programs).
  • Request evidence of impact (e.g., progress data, engagement levels).
December
  • Attend meeting to review the external audit findings.
  • Ensure any recommendations are actioned and statutory obligations are met.
  • Review termly financial report with Finance Committee.
  • Confirm alignment with pupil needs and statutory compliance.

Spring Term

January
  • Meet with SBM and Headteacher to assess budget performance.
  • Identify underspends/overspends and agree reallocation to support pupil needs (e.g., additional teaching assistants for EYFS).
February
  • Review major contracts (e.g., playground maintenance, curriculum resources) for value for money and compliance with procurement rules.
  • Attend Finance Committee meeting to review management accounts.
  • Focus on staffing costs (largest expenditure) and pupil-focused spending (e.g., phonics programs).
  • Meet with SENCO/Deputy Head to evaluate impact of Pupil Premium spending.
  • Ensure disadvantaged pupils' needs are being met effectively (e.g., reading recovery programs).
March
  • Begin discussions with SBM and Headteacher on next year's draft budget.
  • Ensure pupil needs (e.g., EYFS resources, SEND support) are prioritized.
  • Review findings from internal audit (if applicable) and ensure actions are implemented.
April
  • Attend Finance Committee meeting to scrutinize draft budget.
  • Verify alignment with SIP and statutory funding requirements.
  • Review expenditure on SEND provision and ensure compliance with Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs) and statutory duties.

Summer Term

May
  • Review management accounts and forecast year-end position.
  • Ensure no unexpected variances impact pupil provision.
  • Evaluate key spending areas (e.g., utilities, agency staff) for cost-effectiveness.
June
  • Attend Academy Committee meeting to approve final budget for next year.
  • Ensure ESFA submission deadlines are met (by 31 July).
  • Review overall impact of financial decisions on pupil outcomes (e.g., exam results, wellbeing).
July
  • Meet with SBM to review year-end accounts and prepare for ESFA submission.
  • Confirm compliance with Academy Trust Handbook.
  • Verify financial controls (e.g., segregation of duties, bank reconciliations) are robust.
August
  • Review documentation for upcoming external audit.
  • Ensure all financial records are complete and compliant.
  • Reflect on financial oversight for the year and identify areas for improvement.
  • Plan priorities for the next academic year.

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all financial and reporting requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Budgeting and spending are designed to prioritize pupil outcomes and wellbeing.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and committee meetings maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability and transparency.
