Pupil Premium Link Committee Member Timetable

A structured timetable for monitoring pupil premium in a primary school

Below is a detailed timetable for a Pupil Premium Link Committee Member in a UK primary school, designed to ensure effective monitoring and use of pupil premium funding throughout the academic year (September to July). This timetable includes key activities to identify the needs of disadvantaged students, appraise the effectiveness of interventions, and ensure statutory requirements are met.

Autumn Term

September
  • Review and finalise the pupil premium strategy for the academic year, ensuring it aligns with the school's development plan and focuses on foundational skills like literacy and numeracy.
  • Ensure the strategy is published on the school website, as required by statutory guidelines.
  • Meet with the pupil premium coordinator to discuss implementation plans, clarify roles, and confirm priorities.
October
  • Visit the school to observe pupil premium interventions in action (e.g., small group literacy support or numeracy boosters).
  • Meet with staff delivering interventions to understand their responsibilities and identify any training or resource needs.
November
  • Review Autumn term assessment data for pupil premium students (e.g., phonics, reading, or maths outcomes).
  • Compare their progress and attainment with non-pupil premium peers and national benchmarks.
  • Discuss findings with the pupil premium coordinator to identify any adjustments needed to interventions.
December
  • Prepare a concise report for the academy committee, summarising progress and the impact of interventions based on observations and data.
  • Attend the academy committee meeting to present the report and respond to questions or suggestions.

Spring Term

January
  • Ensure any agreed adjustments to interventions from the Autumn term are implemented (e.g., changes to group sizes or focus areas).
  • Observe any new interventions starting in the Spring term.
  • Verify that staff delivering interventions have received appropriate training; attend a session if scheduled.
February
  • Meet with a small focus group of pupil premium students to gather their feedback on the support provided (e.g., what helps them learn best).
  • Optionally, attend a parents' evening to meet with parents of pupil premium students and hear their views.
March
  • Review Spring term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Evaluate the impact of interventions and discuss with the pupil premium coordinator any tweaks needed for the Summer term.
  • Prepare a mid-year report for the academy committee, highlighting progress and areas for improvement.

Summer Term

April
  • Review financial reports to confirm that pupil premium funding is being spent in line with the strategy and delivers value for money.
  • Check that pupil premium eligibility records are accurate and updated (e.g., reflecting new entrants or changes in circumstances).
May
  • Visit the school to observe interventions, focusing on any recent adjustments or new approaches.
  • Meet with staff to discuss progress, challenges, and any additional support they might need.
June
  • Review Summer term assessment data for pupil premium students.
  • Begin drafting an impact evaluation for the year in collaboration with the pupil premium coordinator, focusing on outcomes and lessons learned.
  • Start planning the next academic year's pupil premium strategy based on current data and feedback.
July
  • Finalise the impact evaluation report, assessing the overall effectiveness of the pupil premium strategy for the year.
  • Contribute to developing the next year's pupil premium strategy, incorporating evaluation findings and recommendations.
  • Prepare a final report for the academy committee, summarising the year's impact and outlining future plans.

Ongoing Activities (Throughout the Year)

  • Receive regular updates from the pupil premium coordinator (e.g., monthly emails or brief half-termly meetings) to stay informed of progress and issues.
  • Be available to address urgent matters related to pupil premium funding or support (e.g., unexpected barriers to intervention delivery).
  • Ensure the pupil premium strategy remains aligned with the school's broader goals and is ready for external review (e.g., Ofsted).

Notes

  • Primary School Context: This timetable reflects the primary school focus on foundational skills (e.g., literacy and numeracy) and termly assessment cycles typical of smaller student populations.
  • Flexibility: Adjust the schedule as needed to fit the school's specific assessment dates, committee meetings, or emerging priorities.
  • Statutory Compliance: The timetable ensures key requirements are met, such as publishing the strategy in September and evaluating its impact annually to inform the next cycle.
