Health and Safety Monitoring Timetable

For Academy Committee Link Member (Primary School)

This timetable outlines the responsibilities and schedule for an Academy Committee Link Member to monitor Health and Safety in a UK primary school, ensuring pupil needs are met and statutory requirements are fulfilled. The schedule is structured on a termly basis (Autumn, Spring, Summer) with monthly and ad-hoc tasks, tailored to the needs of younger pupils.

Autumn Term

September
  • Meet with the School Business Manager and Health and Safety (H&S) Lead to review the school's H&S policy and risk assessments.
  • Confirm compliance with statutory requirements (e.g., fire safety, first aid, COSHH).
  • Review previous H&S inspection reports and action plans.
  • Schedule termly walk-throughs and committee meetings.
  • Conduct a site inspection with the H&S Lead to identify hazards (e.g., classroom conditions, playgrounds, dining areas, early years settings).
  • Check pupil-specific needs (e.g., accessibility for SEND pupils, medical plans for pupils with conditions like allergies or epilepsy).
  • Verify fire evacuation routes and signage are clear, accessible, and age-appropriate for young children.
October
  • Confirm staff H&S training records (e.g., paediatric first aid, fire safety, manual handling) are up-to-date.
  • Meet with pastoral team to ensure pupil medical needs (e.g., asthma, allergies, toileting plans) are documented and communicated.
  • Attend a staff briefing to promote H&S awareness and gather feedback, ensuring staff understand needs of younger pupils.
  • Present findings from site walk-through and training review to the Academy Committee.
  • Discuss any outstanding actions and allocate responsibilities.
  • Ensure safeguarding and H&S policies align to protect pupil wellbeing, especially for early years.
November
  • Observe a fire drill to assess evacuation efficiency, ensuring young pupils are supported during the process.
  • Review lockdown procedures and ensure staff/pupils are briefed in an age-appropriate manner.
  • Check first aid kits, defibrillator, and medical supplies (e.g., EpiPens) availability across the site.
  • Sample risk assessments for key areas (e.g., early years, PE, playground activities) to ensure they are current and pupil-focused.
  • Verify off-site trip procedures (e.g., for local walks or visits) comply with DfE guidance and include pupil-specific risk assessments.
December
  • Meet with H&S Lead to evaluate termly progress against statutory requirements.
  • Prepare a termly H&S report for the Academy Committee, highlighting pupil safety outcomes.
  • Plan priorities for Spring Term (e.g., addressing identified hazards in play areas).

Spring Term

January
  • Review updates to national H&S legislation or DfE guidance and ensure school policies reflect these.
  • Check pupil medical records are updated for new starters or changing needs, including early years pupils.
  • Conduct a walk-through focusing on high-risk areas (e.g., dining hall, cloakrooms, early years outdoor areas).
  • Ensure winter-specific hazards (e.g., icy pathways, wet floors) are managed, with attention to pupil safety.
February
  • Meet with the School Council (or equivalent pupil group) to gather age-appropriate feedback on H&S concerns (e.g., safe play areas, feeling secure).
  • Review staff incident reports to identify trends affecting pupil safety, particularly in early years or playtime.
  • Audit staff training completion for new or refreshed H&S requirements, including paediatric first aid.
  • Ensure designated first aiders are available during all school hours, including lunch and playtimes.
March
  • Liaise with external H&S auditors (if scheduled) to prepare documentation.
  • Review accident and near-miss logs (e.g., playground incidents) to ensure proper reporting and follow-up.
  • Report to the Academy Committee on Spring Term progress, including pupil feedback and audit readiness.
  • Discuss budget implications for H&S improvements (e.g., safer playground equipment).
April
  • Evaluate termly H&S performance with the H&S Lead, focusing on pupil outcomes.
  • Prepare a termly report for the Academy Committee.
  • Plan Summer Term priorities (e.g., outdoor safety for warmer weather).

Summer Term

May
  • Inspect outdoor areas (e.g., playgrounds, climbing frames, sandpits) for hazards, ensuring accessibility for all pupils.
  • Verify sun safety measures (e.g., shaded areas, water availability, sun hats policy) are in place for young children.
  • Test fire alarms and emergency lighting systems with the H&S Lead.
  • Ensure pupil-specific evacuation plans (e.g., for SEND or early years pupils) are rehearsed with appropriate support.
June
  • Review risk assessments for upcoming school trips and events (e.g., sports day, school fete).
  • Ensure pupil medical needs (e.g., allergies, mobility) are addressed in event planning.
  • Conduct a walk-through to assess summer-specific risks (e.g., ventilation in classrooms, overheating).

Key Features

  • Statutory Compliance: Ensures all legal requirements are met throughout the year.
  • Pupil Focus: Activities and checks are designed to prioritize pupil safety and wellbeing, especially for younger children.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Regular reviews and site visits maintain high standards.
  • Reporting: Structured reporting to the Academy Committee for accountability.
