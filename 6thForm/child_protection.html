Child Protection Monitoring Timetable

For Academy Committee Link Member in a UK Sixth Form

This timetable outlines the responsibilities and schedule for monitoring child protection, ensuring pupil needs are met, staff are appraised, and statutory requirements under UK legislation (e.g., Keeping Children Safe in Education, Working Together to Safeguard Children) are fulfilled. The timetable is structured for a four-term academic year (Autumn, Winter, Spring, Summer).

Termly Tasks (4 Times per Year: Autumn, Winter, Spring, Summer)

1. Review Safeguarding Policies and Procedures

  • Meet with the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) to review the Sixth Form's safeguarding and child protection policy.
  • Ensure alignment with the latest Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) guidance and local safeguarding partnership requirements.
  • Verify that policies are accessible to staff, students, and parents.
  • Time: Early in each term (September, January, April, June).
  • Duration: 2 hours.

2. Scrutinise Safeguarding Training Compliance

  • Confirm all staff (including temporary and supply staff) have completed mandatory safeguarding training as per KCSIE.
  • Review training records and discuss refresher training needs with the DSL.
  • Check that training covers key areas: child protection, online safety, PREVENT, and peer-on-peer abuse.
  • Time: Mid-term (October, February, May, July).
  • Duration: 1.5 hours.

3. Monitor Child Protection Case Oversight

  • Review anonymised records of safeguarding concerns and actions taken, ensuring timely interventions and referrals to external agencies (e.g., Local Authority Designated Officer, social services).
  • Verify that pupil needs are being met through individual support plans, where applicable.
  • Discuss any trends or recurring issues with the DSL and senior leadership team (SLT).
  • Time: End of term (December, March, May, August).
  • Duration: 2 hours.

4. Attend Academy Committee Meeting

  • Present a child protection monitoring report to the committee, highlighting compliance, concerns, and recommendations.
  • Ensure safeguarding is a standing agenda item and that the committee is informed of statutory duties.
  • Time: As per committee schedule (typically end of term).
  • Duration: 1 hour.

Monthly Tasks

1. Meet with Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

  • Conduct a brief meeting to discuss ongoing safeguarding concerns, updates on cases, and any changes to statutory guidance.
  • Review the Single Central Record (SCR) to ensure all staff and volunteers have up-to-date DBS checks and qualifications.
  • Time: First week of each month.
  • Duration: 1 hour.

2. Observe Safeguarding Culture

  • Conduct a walk-through of the Sixth Form to observe safeguarding practices (e.g., staff-student interactions, signage about reporting concerns).
  • Engage informally with students to gauge awareness of safeguarding procedures and how to raise concerns.
  • Time: Mid-month.
  • Duration: 1 hour.

3. Check Pastoral Support Systems

  • Review the effectiveness of pastoral care systems (e.g., tutoring, mental health support, anti-bullying measures) to ensure pupil needs are met.
  • Confirm that students with specific vulnerabilities (e.g., SEND, looked-after children) have tailored support plans.
  • Time: Last week of each month.
  • Duration: 1.5 hours.

Ad-Hoc Tasks (As Needed)

1. Respond to Serious Incidents

  • If a serious safeguarding incident occurs, meet with the DSL and SLT within 48 hours to review the response and ensure compliance with statutory reporting (e.g., to the Local Safeguarding Children Partnership or LADO).
  • Verify that lessons learned are documented and actioned.
  • Duration: Varies (typically 1-2 hours).

2. Support Staff Appraisals

  • Liaise with the DSL and HR lead to ensure safeguarding responsibilities are embedded in staff appraisals, particularly for pastoral and leadership roles.
  • Confirm that staff demonstrate competence in safeguarding through performance reviews.
  • Time: During appraisal cycle (usually annually or biannually).
  • Duration: 1 hour per review cycle.

3. Engage with External Reviews

  • Participate in or review findings from external safeguarding audits or inspections (e.g., Ofsted, local authority reviews).
  • Ensure any recommendations are implemented promptly and monitored.
  • Time: As scheduled by external bodies.
  • Duration: Varies (2-4 hours).

Key Notes

  • All activities must comply with Keeping Children Safe in Education (updated annually) and local safeguarding procedures.
  • Maintain confidentiality and GDPR compliance when handling sensitive information.
  • Ensure regular communication with the DSL and SLT to address emerging issues promptly.
  • Record all monitoring activities in a log for accountability and to inform committee reports.
  • Allocate approximately 10-12 hours per term for scheduled tasks, with flexibility for ad-hoc responsibilities.

This timetable ensures robust oversight of child protection, prioritises pupil welfare, and meets statutory requirements while supporting staff development and accountability.
