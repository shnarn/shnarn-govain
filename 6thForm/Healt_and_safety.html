Health and Safety Monitoring Timetable for Academy Committee Link Member (6th Form)

This timetable outlines the responsibilities and schedule for an Academy Committee Link Member tasked with monitoring Health and Safety (H&S) in a 6th Form, ensuring pupil needs are met, appraisals are conducted, monitoring is consistent, and all statutory requirements are fulfilled in line with current UK legislation.

Weekly Responsibilities (Ongoing)

  • Review Incident Reports: Check accident/incident logs via the school's H&S reporting system (e.g., Every or similar software) for trends or unresolved issues. Ensure incidents involving pupils are followed up with appropriate actions and communicated to parents where necessary.
  • Liaise with H&S Officer: Brief weekly check-in (email or 15-minute meeting) with the designated H&S Officer to discuss ongoing concerns, risk assessments, and pupil-specific needs (e.g., medical conditions, accessibility requirements).
  • Pupil Voice: Coordinate with the Student Council to gather feedback on H&S concerns (e.g., building safety, mental health provisions) every 2 weeks. Ensure pupil needs, particularly for SEND or vulnerable students, are addressed.

Monthly Responsibilities

  • Site Walk: Conduct a 1-hour walk-through with the Site Manager to inspect key areas (classrooms, common rooms, science labs, sports facilities, fire exits). Verify compliance with statutory requirements (e.g., fire safety, COSHH regulations).
    • Checklist: Clear fire exits, functional fire alarms, safe storage of hazardous materials, adequate lighting, and ventilation.
  • Review Risk Assessments: Audit a sample of risk assessments (e.g., for practical subjects like science, DT, or PE) to ensure they are up-to-date and address pupil-specific needs (e.g., allergies, mobility issues).
  • Training Compliance: Confirm with HR that staff H&S training (e.g., fire safety, first aid) is current. Ensure at least 2 trained first aiders are available during school hours, as per DfE guidance.

Termly Responsibilities

Autumn Term (September–December)

  • H&S Policy Review: Review the school’s H&S policy with the Senior Leadership Team (SLT) to ensure alignment with HSE and DfE guidelines. Confirm updates reflect pupil needs (e.g., mental health support, accessibility).
  • Emergency Drills: Observe and evaluate a fire drill or lockdown practice. Verify all pupils, including those with SEND, are accounted for and evacuation procedures meet statutory standards.
  • Committee Reporting: Present a termly H&S report to the Academy Committee, summarizing incidents, pupil feedback, and compliance status. Highlight any areas needing investment (e.g., repairs, equipment).

Spring Term (January–March)

  • External Audit Coordination: Arrange for an external H&S audit (e.g., by a local authority or independent consultant) to verify compliance with statutory requirements. Review findings and ensure action plans are created for any issues.
  • Pupil Needs Assessment: Meet with the SENDCo and pastoral team to review Individual Healthcare Plans (IHPs) and Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs). Ensure H&S measures (e.g., medical storage, evacuation plans) are tailored to pupil needs.
  • Appraisal Input: Provide feedback to SLT on H&S Officer’s performance based on monitoring outcomes. Suggest training or process improvements if needed.

Summer Term (April–July)

  • Annual H&S Review: Conduct a comprehensive review of the year’s H&S performance, including incident trends, pupil feedback, and compliance with statutory requirements. Propose updates to policies or procedures for the next academic year.
  • Premises Maintenance Check: Work with the Site Manager to ensure summer maintenance (e.g., asbestos checks, electrical testing) is scheduled and complies with HSE regulations.
  • Pupil Transition Planning: Review H&S provisions for incoming Year 12 students (e.g., induction on safety protocols, medical needs documentation). Ensure transition plans address pupil-specific requirements.

Key Statutory Requirements Addressed

  • Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (last amended 2015): Ensures a safe environment for pupils and staff.
  • Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 (last amended 2003): Requires regular risk assessments and competent H&S advice.
  • Equality Act 2010 (last amended 2020): Mandates reasonable adjustments for SEND pupils and accessibility.
  • Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) 2024: Aligns H&S measures with safeguarding requirements, ensuring pupil welfare.
  • Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992 (last amended 2002): Covers workplace conditions like lighting and ventilation.
  • Control of Substances Hazardous to Health (COSHH) Regulations 2002: Governs safe handling of hazardous materials.
  • Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005: Mandates fire safety measures, including drills and risk assessments.
  • Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations (RIDDOR) 2013: Requires reporting of serious incidents.
  • Health and Safety (First-Aid) Regulations 1981 (last amended 2013): Ensures adequate first aid provision.

Monitoring and Appraisal

  • Data Tracking: Use a spreadsheet or school MIS to track incidents, training completion, and risk assessment updates. Share summaries with SLT and the Academy Committee.
  • Appraisal Contribution: Provide termly feedback on H&S Officer and Site Manager performance to SLT, focusing on responsiveness, compliance, and pupil-centered outcomes.
  • Pupil-Centered Focus: Regularly consult pupils (via surveys or Student Council) to ensure their H&S needs are met, particularly for mental health and physical accessibility.

Notes

  • Schedule meetings and walks to minimize disruption to teaching (e.g., during free periods or after school).
  • Maintain confidentiality for pupil-specific data in line with GDPR and school policies.
  • Escalate urgent H&S issues (e.g., structural hazards) to the Headteacher and Academy Committee immediately.
