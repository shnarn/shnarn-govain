TIMETABLE FOR ACADEMY COMMITTEE LINK MEMBER: MONITORING SEND IN A UK SIXTH FORM

This timetable outlines the responsibilities of an Academy Committee Link Member tasked with monitoring Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in a UK sixth form, ensuring compliance with the SEND Code of Practice (2015).

Autumn Term (September - December)

September
Week 1-2: Initial Meeting with SENDCo
  • Review the SEND register and individual Education, Health, and Care Plans (EHCPs).
  • Discuss the sixth form’s SEND policy and compliance with the SEND Code of Practice (2015).
  • Confirm arrangements for monitoring pupil progress and staff training.
  • Action: Request a copy of the SEND Information Report and check it aligns with statutory requirements (published annually on the school website).
Week 3-4: Classroom Observations
  • Observe lessons to evaluate the implementation of SEND support strategies (e.g., differentiated teaching, reasonable adjustments).
  • Focus on students with EHCPs and those receiving SEND support.
  • Action: Document findings and discuss with SENDCo to ensure adjustments meet pupil needs.
October
Week 1: Review Pupil Progress
  • Meet with SENDCo to review termly progress data for SEND students (e.g., academic attainment, attendance, engagement).
  • Check that interventions are in place for underperforming students.
  • Action: Ensure progress reviews are recorded and shared with relevant staff/parents.
Week 3: Parent/Carer Engagement
  • Attend a meeting with parents/carers of SEND students to gather feedback on support provision.
  • Verify that parents are informed of their child’s progress and involved in EHCP reviews.
  • Action: Confirm compliance with statutory requirement for annual EHCP reviews.
November
Week 2: Staff Training Audit
  • Review staff training records to ensure all teachers and support staff have received SEND training.
  • Check that training addresses specific needs (e.g., autism, dyslexia) relevant to the cohort.
  • Action: Recommend additional training if gaps are identified.
December
Week 1: Policy and Compliance Check
  • Review the SEND policy and accessibility plan to ensure they meet statutory requirements (Equality Act 2010, Children and Families Act 2014).
  • Action: Report findings to the Academy Committee and suggest updates if needed.
Week 3: Termly Report
  • Prepare a report for the Academy Committee summarizing SEND monitoring activities, pupil outcomes, and compliance.
  • Action: Present report at committee meeting and address any concerns raised.

Spring Term (January - March)

January
Week 2: EHCP Review Monitoring
  • Attend a sample of EHCP review meetings to ensure they are conducted in line with statutory guidance.
  • Verify that student and parent voices are included in the review process.
  • Action: Provide feedback to SENDCo and ensure actions from reviews are implemented.
February
Week 1-2: Learning Walk
  • Conduct a learning walk with SENDCo to observe the impact of SEND support in lessons (e.g., use of assistive technology, teaching assistant support).
  • Action: Record observations and discuss improvements with SENDCo.
Week 3: Transition Planning
  • Review transition plans for Year 12 students moving to Year 13, and Year 13 students preparing for post-18 destinations (e.g., university, employment).
  • Ensure plans address SEND-specific needs (e.g., access arrangements for exams, career advice).
  • Action: Confirm that transition plans are documented and shared with relevant stakeholders.
March
Week 1: Safeguarding and SEND
  • Meet with the Designated Safeguarding Lead to ensure SEND students’ safeguarding needs are met.
  • Check that risk assessments for SEND students (e.g., those with mobility issues) are in place.
  • Action: Report any concerns to the Academy Committee.
Week 3: Termly Report
  • Prepare a termly report for the Academy Committee on SEND provision, focusing on progress, interventions, and compliance.
  • Action: Present report and discuss any emerging issues.

Summer Term (April - July)

April
Week 2: Exam Access Arrangements
  • Review the process for identifying and implementing exam access arrangements (e.g., extra time, scribes) for SEND students.
  • Verify compliance with Joint Council for Qualifications (JCQ) regulations.
  • Action: Ensure all eligible students have appropriate arrangements in place.
May
Week 1: Pupil Voice
  • Meet with a sample of SEND students to gather feedback on their support and sixth form experience.
  • Action: Use feedback to inform improvements in provision and share findings with SENDCo.
June
Week 2: Annual SEND Report Review
  • Review the updated SEND Information Report for publication on the school website.
  • Ensure it reflects current provision and meets statutory requirements.
  • Action: Approve the report or suggest revisions.
Week 3: External Agency Engagement
  • Meet with external agencies (e.g., educational psychologists, local authority SEND team) to review their involvement in supporting SEND students.
  • Action: Ensure effective collaboration and follow-up on recommendations.
July
Week 1: Annual Evaluation
  • Conduct an annual review of SEND provision with SENDCo and senior leadership.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of interventions, staff training, and pupil outcomes.
  • Action: Prepare a comprehensive report for the Academy Committee.
Week 2: Planning for Next Year
  • Discuss priorities for the next academic year, including updates to the SEND policy, staff training, and resource allocation.
  • Action: Agree on an action plan and present to the Academy Committee.

Ongoing Responsibilities

  • Monthly Check-Ins: Brief meetings with SENDCo to stay updated on emerging issues, pupil progress, and compliance.
  • Governor Training: Attend SEND-related training to stay informed of legal and best practice updates.
  • Record Keeping: Maintain accurate records of all monitoring activities, meetings, and findings for accountability and Ofsted inspections.
  • Liaison with Academy Committee: Regularly update the committee on SEND matters and escalate concerns as needed.

Notes

  • All activities must align with the SEND Code of Practice (2015), Equality Act 2010, and Children and Families Act 2014.
  • Ensure confidentiality and data protection compliance when handling pupil information.
  • Collaborate with the SENDCo, senior leadership, and external agencies to maintain high standards of SEND provision.
  • Adjust the timetable as needed based on the sixth form’s specific calendar and needs.
