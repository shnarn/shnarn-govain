Sixth Form Behaviour and Attendance Monitoring Timetable

For Academy Committee Link Member
Aligned with UK Statutory Requirements and Student Needs
Four-Term Calendar (Autumn, Winter, Spring, Summer)

Autumn Term (September–December)

Weeks 1–2
  • Review attendance data from previous academic year using MIS (e.g., SIMS, Arbor) to establish baseline.
  • Meet pastoral lead to assess behaviour policies and support plans for students with SEND (SEND Code of Practice 2015, Education Act 2002).
  • Conduct learning walk in communal areas (e.g., common room, library) to evaluate student conduct and safeguarding measures.
Weeks 3–4
  • Observe Year 12/13 lessons (one per week, rotating subjects) using behaviour checklist aligned with school policy.
  • Meet attendance officer to identify persistent absentees (below 90% attendance) and verify escalation processes (DfE Attendance Guidance 2022).
  • Check safeguarding logs for behaviour/attendance concerns (Keeping Children Safe in Education 2024).
Weeks 5–6
  • Hold student voice session with diverse group (e.g., SEND, disadvantaged, LAC) to gather feedback on behaviour policies.
  • Review behaviour logs for trends and intervention effectiveness (e.g., behaviour contracts, pastoral plans).
  • Verify exclusion/suspension records comply with DfE guidance (Suspension and Permanent Exclusion 2023).
Weeks 7–8
  • Meet SLT to discuss termly findings and appraise behaviour/attendance strategies.
  • Prepare termly report for academy committee, summarising attendance rates, behaviour trends, and compliance status.
  • Ensure alternative provision for excluded students meets statutory requirements.

Winter Term (January–March)

Weeks 1–2
  • Analyse updated attendance data, focusing on changes since Autumn term.
  • Meet pastoral lead to review progress on SEND/EHCP support plans and mental health provisions.
  • Conduct learning walk during non-lesson time to assess adherence to dress code and behaviour expectations.
Weeks 3–4
  • Observe lessons (one per week, alternating Year 12/13) to monitor engagement and teacher interventions.
  • Review parental contact records for attendance/behaviour issues, ensuring timely communication (Equality Act 2010).
  • Check safeguarding compliance for vulnerable students (e.g., LAC, those with CP plans).
Weeks 5–6
  • Hold student voice session with new group to ensure broad representation and feedback.
  • Verify that behaviour interventions align with student needs and Ofsted expectations.
  • Review attendance escalation actions (e.g., letters, meetings) for persistent absentees.
Weeks 7–8
  • Meet SLT to evaluate termly progress and suggest policy adjustments.
  • Prepare termly report for academy committee, highlighting pupil needs and statutory compliance.
  • Attend governor meeting to present findings and ensure governance aligns with DfE/Ofsted standards.

Spring Term (April–June)

Weeks 1–2
  • Review termly attendance data, focusing on trends for disadvantaged or SEND students.
  • Meet attendance officer to assess intervention impact on persistent absentees.
  • Conduct learning walk to monitor behaviour in communal areas and verify safeguarding measures.
Weeks 3–4
  • Observe lessons (one per week, rotating subjects) to ensure consistent behaviour management.
  • Check behaviour logs and support plan updates for students on pastoral or behaviour contracts.
  • Verify compliance with exclusion guidance and alternative provision arrangements.
Weeks 5–6
  • Hold student voice session to gather feedback on pastoral support and behaviour policies.
  • Review parental engagement records, ensuring support for families meets statutory requirements.
  • Analyse safeguarding logs for any unresolved behaviour/attendance concerns.
Weeks 7–8
  • Meet SLT to discuss termly findings and appraise intervention effectiveness.
  • Prepare termly report for academy committee, focusing on pupil progress and compliance.
  • Ensure GDPR compliance in all data handling and reporting.

Summer Term (June–August)

Weeks 1–2
  • Review full-year attendance data to identify long-term trends and areas for improvement.
  • Meet pastoral lead to evaluate SEND/EAL support and mental health provisions.
  • Conduct final learning walk to assess behaviour and safeguarding before summer break.
Weeks 3–4
  • Observe lessons (one per week, focusing on Year 13 transitions) to monitor engagement.
  • Verify that all statutory requirements (e.g., exclusions, safeguarding) are met for year-end.
  • Review parental contact logs for unresolved attendance/behaviour issues.
Weeks 5–6
  • Hold final student voice session to gather feedback for next academic year’s planning.
  • Analyse behaviour data to assess policy impact and recommend updates.
  • Ensure alternative provision records are complete and compliant with DfE guidance.
Weeks 7–8
  • Meet SLT to finalise annual appraisal of behaviour/attendance strategies.
  • Prepare annual report for academy committee, summarising pupil needs, trends, and compliance.
  • Attend governor meeting to present findings and inform strategic planning for next year.

Additional Notes

  • Rotate student voice groups each term to ensure inclusivity (e.g., Year 12/13, diverse demographics).
  • Conduct deep-dive data reviews annually to inform strategic planning.
  • Maintain confidentiality and GDPR compliance in all data handling.
  • Ensure all activities align with Ofsted expectations and statutory duties (Education Act 1996, Equality Act 2010).
