Academy Committee Link Member Expectations for Monitoring 6th Form Subjects

Role Overview

The Link Member provides governance oversight for 6th Form subjects, ensuring high-quality education, pupil progress, and compliance with statutory requirements (e.g., DfE curriculum standards, SEND Code of Practice, safeguarding). The expectations focus on monitoring teaching quality, pupil needs, and statutory compliance while collaborating with the Senior Leadership Team (SLT), subject leads, and pastoral staff. The academic year is divided into four terms (Autumn, Winter, Spring, Summer), with tasks distributed to maintain consistent oversight without micromanaging operational duties.

Assumptions

  • The 6th Form offers A-Levels, BTECs, or similar qualifications.
  • Monitoring covers academic progress, teaching quality, pupil support (e.g., SEND, disadvantaged students), and statutory compliance.
  • Time commitment is approximately 2-4 hours per week, with additional time for termly reviews.
  • The Link Member acts as a critical friend, providing oversight and challenge, not operational management.

Weekly Expectations (Across All Terms)

  • Data Review: Regularly analyze subject performance data (e.g., progress reports, mock exam results, attendance) provided by SLT or subject leads. Focus on pupil progress, attainment gaps (e.g., SEND, disadvantaged students), and trends compared to national benchmarks. Follow up with SLT via email or meetings to clarify data or request interventions for underperforming pupils.
  • Subject Engagement: Meet with subject leads (rotate subjects, e.g., Maths, English, Science) to discuss curriculum delivery, assessment methods, and pupil engagement. Review schemes of work and assessments to ensure alignment with A-Level/BTEC specifications and inclusivity requirements (Equality Act 2010).
  • Lesson Observations: Conduct learning walks (at least once per term) to observe teaching quality, pupil engagement, and SEND support. Coordinate with SLT to minimize disruption and document findings against Ofsted criteria (e.g., quality of education, behaviour).
  • Pupil Support Oversight: Engage with pastoral leads or SEND coordinators (biweekly recommended) to review support for pupils with additional needs (e.g., EHCPs, mental health). Ensure subjects accommodate these needs per the SEND Code of Practice.
  • Pupil Voice: Review feedback from pupil surveys or student council to assess satisfaction and engagement in subjects. Address concerns raised by pupils or parents (via SLT) in line with academy policies.
  • Reporting: Prepare concise reports for monthly Academy Committee meetings, summarizing findings on subject performance, pupil needs, and statutory compliance. Highlight good practice and areas for improvement.
  • Safeguarding Check: During all activities (e.g., learning walks, meetings), verify safeguarding practices (e.g., staff awareness, pupil safety) align with Keeping Children Safe in Education.

Termly Expectations (Four-Term Structure)

Autumn Term (September–October)

  • Review the 6th Form improvement plan and subject-specific priorities with SLT.
  • Check updated policies (e.g., curriculum, safeguarding, assessment) for compliance with DfE and Ofsted standards.
  • Schedule subject lead meetings and learning walks for the year.
  • Conduct an in-depth review of one subject (e.g., Maths), analyzing curriculum, teaching, and pupil outcomes.
  • Ensure safeguarding training is up to date (mandatory annual requirement).

Winter Term (November–December)

  • Monitor interim assessment data (e.g., predicted grades) to identify underperformance or gaps.
  • Observe appraisals (as permitted) to ensure teacher performance supports pupil progress.
  • Review careers guidance provision to confirm alignment with Gatsby Benchmarks.
  • Conduct a learning walk focused on pupil engagement and behaviour across subjects.
  • Verify safeguarding audits and staff training records are current.

Spring Term (January–March)

  • Conduct an in-depth review of a second subject (e.g., English), focusing on curriculum delivery and inclusivity.
  • Review external exam entries and access arrangements for SEND pupils to ensure compliance with JCQ regulations.
  • Meet with pastoral leads to evaluate mental health and SEND support effectiveness.
  • Analyze pupil voice feedback to identify subject-specific concerns or successes.
  • Prepare a mid-year report for the Academy Committee, summarizing progress and compliance.

Summer Term (April–July)

  • Conduct an in-depth review of a third subject (e.g., Science).
  • Analyze final exam results and destinations data (e.g., university, apprenticeships) against national benchmarks and academy targets.
  • Evaluate the 6th Form’s overall performance, focusing on pupil outcomes and curriculum effectiveness.
  • Contribute to the annual governance report, ensuring evidence of all statutory duties (e.g., curriculum, safeguarding, SEND).
  • Review end-of-year pupil feedback and parent/carer correspondence for trends.

Annual Expectations

  • Statutory Compliance: Verify the 6th Form meets all DfE requirements (e.g., curriculum coverage, safeguarding audits, SEND provision).
  • Training: Complete mandatory governance and safeguarding training to stay compliant with Academies Financial Handbook and KCSIE.
  • Exam Oversight: Ensure exam processes (e.g., entries, access arrangements) comply with JCQ and DfE regulations.
  • Destinations and Careers: Confirm robust careers guidance meets statutory Gatsby Benchmarks and supports pupil transitions.
  • Reporting: Contribute to the annual governance report, evidencing oversight of pupil outcomes, teaching quality, and statutory duties.

Notes

  • Flexibility: Adapt tasks to align with academy schedules or the Link Member’s availability.
  • Confidentiality: Handle all data (e.g., pupil progress, appraisals) per GDPR and academy policies.
  • Escalation: Raise concerns (e.g., non-compliance, pupil underperformance) to the Academy Committee Chair promptly.
  • Collaboration: Work closely with SLT and subject leads, maintaining a governance-focused role.
